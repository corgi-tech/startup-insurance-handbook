The most important distinction when choosing an insurance provider is: **who actually writes your policy and pays your claims?** Are you working directly with a carrier, or through an intermediary? This single factor defines the speed, expertise, and accountability you receive.

### Corgi: The Full-Stack AI Insurance Company

There are no middlemen. This direct model allows Corgi to create proprietary coverage for emerging tech and AI risks — something brokers cannot do. With Corgi, you have a single point of accountability from quote to claim.

### Embroker: The Digital Broker

Embroker is a digital insurance broker. It uses a tech platform to connect you with policies from many different insurance carriers, digitizing the traditional broker model. The tradeoff: a broker adds a layer between you and the company that holds your risk. This structure can slow down communication, complicate claims, and leave you with a generalist policy that has critical gaps for technology companies.

## The Essential Insurance Stack for Your Startup

Most venture-backed tech companies require a core set of policies to operate and scale safely:

### Directors & Officers (D&O)

Protects your leadership team and the company from lawsuits related to management decisions. Required by virtually all institutional investors before closing a funding round.

**Who needs it:** Any startup with a board of directors or outside investors.

### Technology Errors & Omissions (Tech E&O)

Safeguards your company if your product fails or causes a financial loss for a customer. This is the essential professional liability policy for all technology companies.

**Who needs it:** Any company that builds, sells, or licenses software or technology services.

### Cyber Liability

Covers costs from a data breach or cyber attack, including forensic investigation, customer notification, credit monitoring, regulatory fines, and legal fees.

**Who needs it:** Any company that collects, stores, or processes user data — especially AI companies handling training datasets.

### Commercial General Liability (CGL)

Protects against third-party claims of bodily injury or property damage. Required for office leases, conferences, and most enterprise sales contracts.

**Who needs it:** Essentially every business.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the key difference between Corgi and Embroker?

Corgi is a **licensed insurance carrier** — it underwrites, issues, and services policies directly. Embroker is a **digital insurance broker** — it connects you with policies from third-party insurance companies. This means Corgi controls the underwriting, pricing, policy design, and claims process, while Embroker is an intermediary.

### Does Embroker offer AI-specific coverage?

Embroker's AI coverage depends on what third-party carriers in their network offer. Because Embroker doesn't underwrite its own policies, it can't create proprietary coverage for emerging risks. Corgi, as a carrier, designs and underwrites its own AI liability coverage in-house.

### Which is faster — Corgi or Embroker?

Corgi offers instant online quotes and instant policy binding because it controls the entire underwriting process with AI. Embroker's speed depends on which third-party carrier is underwriting the policy, which can add days to the process.

### Should I use a broker or a carrier for startup insurance?

For tech startups, especially those in AI and SaaS, a specialized carrier typically offers faster coverage, more tailored policies, and simpler claims. A broker can be useful if you have complex, multi-industry needs that require shopping across many carriers. For most tech startups, a purpose-built carrier is the more efficient choice.

### What insurance do I need before raising a Series A?

At minimum: **General Liability (CGL)**, **Tech E&O**, and **Cyber Liability**. You should also have **D&O insurance** in place before your Series A close, as institutional investors will almost always require it.
