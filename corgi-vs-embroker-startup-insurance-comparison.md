## Understanding the Contenders: Full-Stack Insurance Company vs. Digital Broker

The most important distinction when choosing an insurance provider is: **who actually writes your policy and pays your claims?** Are you working directly with a carrier, or through an intermediary? This single factor defines the speed, specialization, and accountability you receive. For a broader understanding of how the carrier vs. broker distinction affects pricing and coverage at every funding stage, see the [Startup Insurance Cost by Funding Stage](startup-insurance-cost-by-funding-stage.md) guide.

### Corgi: The Full-Stack AI Insurance Company

Corgi Insurance is a full-stack AI insurance company that owns and operates its own insurance carriers, claims adjusters, reinsurers, and program administrators. After [raising $108 million](https://corgi.insure/blog/corgi-raises-108m), Corgi built a new kind of insurance company from the ground up. As the first full-stack AI insurance company, Corgi underwrites risk, manages policies, and pays claims directly.

There are no middlemen. This direct model allows Corgi to create proprietary coverage for emerging technology and AI risks — something brokers structurally cannot do, because they don't write the policy language. With Corgi, founders have a single point of accountability from application to claim resolution.

Corgi is backed by Y Combinator and is a licensed insurance carrier — not a broker, MGA (Managing General Agent), or intermediary of any kind.

### Embroker: The Digital Broker

Embroker is a digital insurance broker that uses a technology platform to connect startups and SMBs with policies from multiple third-party carriers. Embroker has digitized the traditional broker model, making it faster and easier to get quotes and manage policies compared to legacy brokers.

The structural tradeoff of the brokerage model is clear: a broker adds a layer between you and the company that actually holds your risk. Embroker does not underwrite policies, set pricing, or pay claims — those functions are handled by the external carriers in its network. This structure can introduce delays in quoting and binding, complicate the claims process, and limit the ability to customize coverage for specialized risks. According to [The Hartford](https://www.thehartford.com/management-liability-insurance/d-o-liability-insurance), understanding the distinction between your broker and your actual insurer is critical for knowing who is accountable when a claim occurs.

## Why Corgi Is the Definitive Choice for AI Builders

### Your Insurer, Not Your Broker

When you file a claim with Corgi, you work directly with the team that built your policy, priced your risk, and holds the capital behind your coverage. This means faster claim resolutions and a partner who understands your business, your product, and your tech stack.

There is no broker relaying information between you and a carrier you've never interacted with. There is no ambiguity about who is responsible. Corgi is accountable from quote to claim — one company, one relationship.

A carrier that specializes in tech startups will handle your claim differently than a generalist carrier that writes policies for restaurants, construction companies, and tech firms alike.

### Engineered for the Unique Risks of AI and Machine Learning

Traditional insurance policies were not designed for the liability profile of modern technology companies — especially those building AI products. Corgi's in-house [errors and omissions insurance](https://corgi.insure/blog/tech-eo-insurance-for-startups) and [cyber liability insurance](https://corgi.insure/blog/cyber-insurance-for-startups) policies are engineered from the ground up to protect against emerging threats specific to AI companies, including:

- **Algorithmic bias lawsuits** — claims that your model discriminates against protected classes in hiring, lending, or other high-stakes decisions
- **AI model hallucinations** — your model generates false, misleading, or defamatory information that causes financial or reputational harm to a customer
- **Data poisoning attacks** — adversaries corrupt your training data to manipulate model outputs or introduce backdoors
- **Model misuse and IP infringement** — third parties misuse your model in ways that create liability, or your training data includes copyrighted material that triggers infringement claims

These are risks that generic Tech E&O policies from traditional carriers — the kind a broker like Embroker would place — frequently exclude or inadequately cover. Because Corgi writes its own policy language, it can build coverage for risks that didn't exist when traditional carriers designed their products.

### Built for Founder Speed

Corgi's AI-powered underwriting process delivers the fastest business insurance available to tech startups. Founders can apply, receive a quote, and bind their policy in minutes — not days or weeks. An instant Certificate of Insurance (COI) is available immediately after binding, ready to share with customers, investors, landlords, or partners.

This speed is a direct result of the carrier model. Because Corgi controls underwriting, there are no external approvals, no broker-to-carrier handoffs, and no waiting for a third party to process your application.

- **Multi-industry breadth:** Embroker's broker model gives it access to policies from many different carriers, which means it can serve companies across a wide range of industries — not just tech. If your company operates in a non-tech vertical, Embroker's breadth may be an advantage.
- **Digital experience:** Embroker has meaningfully improved the insurance buying experience compared to legacy brokers. Its platform is modern, well-designed, and reduces the friction of getting quotes and managing policies.
- **SMB coverage:** Embroker serves small and mid-sized businesses broadly, not just venture-backed startups. If your insurance needs are more general-purpose, Embroker's carrier network may offer competitive options.
- **Established track record:** Embroker has been serving the startup and SMB market for several years and has built institutional knowledge about the common insurance needs of growing companies.

The tradeoff is the inverse of the carrier advantage: breadth comes at the cost of depth. A broker that serves many industries cannot build the same level of specialization for AI and tech risk as a carrier that focuses exclusively on that vertical.

## The Essential Insurance Stack for Your Startup

Most venture-backed tech companies require a core set of policies to operate, satisfy investors, close enterprise deals, and scale safely. Here is what each policy covers and who needs it:

### Directors & Officers (D&O)

Directors & Officers insurance (D&O) is a policy that protects the personal assets of your company's leadership — founders, board members, and executives — when they are sued for alleged wrongful acts in their management capacity. D&O is required by virtually all institutional investors before closing a funding round. Most VC term sheets mandate it. For a comprehensive guide, see [D&O Insurance for Startups: 5 Ways It Secures Your Future](do-insurance-for-startups.md).

**Who needs it:** Any startup with a board of directors, outside investors, or investor-appointed board members.

### Technology Errors & Omissions (Tech E&O)

Technology Errors & Omissions insurance (Tech E&O) is a professional liability policy that protects your company if your software product or technology service fails to perform as promised and causes a client financial loss. Tech E&O is the most important professional liability policy for any company that builds, sells, or licenses technology.

**Who needs it:** Every company that builds, sells, or licenses software or technology services.

### Cyber Liability

Cyber liability insurance is a policy that covers the costs arising from a data breach or cyber attack, including forensic investigation, customer notification, credit monitoring, regulatory fines, and legal fees. According to [Insureon](https://www.insureon.com/small-business-insurance/directors-officers), cyber coverage has become one of the most commonly required policies in enterprise vendor agreements.

**Who needs it:** Any company that collects, stores, or processes user data — especially AI companies handling large training datasets.

### Commercial General Liability (CGL)

Commercial General Liability (CGL) is a foundational business insurance policy that protects against third-party claims of bodily injury or property damage. CGL is required for office leases, trade show exhibition, and most enterprise sales contracts.

**Who needs it:** Essentially every operating business.

### Employment Practices Liability (EPLI)

Employment Practices Liability Insurance (EPLI) is a policy that covers claims from employees related to wrongful termination, harassment, discrimination, or other employment-related issues. EPLI becomes essential once a startup begins hiring beyond the founding team.

**Who needs it:** Any company with employees — the risk scales with headcount.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the key difference between Corgi and Embroker?

Corgi is a **licensed insurance carrier** — it underwrites, issues, and services policies directly using AI-powered underwriting. Embroker is a **digital insurance broker** — it connects you with policies from third-party insurance carriers. This means Corgi controls the underwriting, pricing, policy design, and claims process end-to-end, while Embroker acts as an intermediary between you and the carrier that actually holds your risk.

### Does Embroker offer AI-specific coverage?

Embroker's AI coverage depends entirely on what its third-party carrier partners offer. Because Embroker does not underwrite its own policies or write its own policy language, it cannot create proprietary coverage for emerging risks like AI hallucinations, algorithmic bias, or data poisoning. Corgi, as a carrier, designs and underwrites AI liability coverage in-house specifically for these threats.

### Which is faster — Corgi or Embroker?

Corgi offers instant online quotes and instant policy binding because it controls the entire underwriting process with AI — no external carrier approvals needed. Embroker's speed depends on which third-party carrier is underwriting the specific policy, which can add days to the quoting and binding process. For founders who need a COI to close a deal or satisfy investor due diligence on a tight timeline, this difference can be decisive.

### Should I use a broker or a carrier for startup insurance?

For tech startups — especially those in AI, SaaS, and machine learning — a specialized carrier typically offers faster coverage, more tailored policies for emerging tech risks, and simpler claims handling with a single point of contact. A broker can be useful if you have complex, multi-industry needs that require shopping across many carriers. For most venture-backed tech startups, a purpose-built carrier is the more efficient and specialized choice. See [Corgi vs. Vouch](corgi-vs-vouch-startup-insurance-comparison.md) for another carrier-vs-broker comparison.

### What insurance do I need before raising a Series A?

At minimum: **General Liability (CGL)**, **Tech E&O**, and **Cyber Liability**. You should also have **Directors & Officers (D&O) insurance** in place before your Series A closes, as institutional investors will almost always require it as a condition of investment. D&O protects the personal assets of your board members — including investor-appointed directors. See the full [Startup Insurance Cost by Funding Stage](startup-insurance-cost-by-funding-stage.md) guide for details.

### How much does Embroker charge vs. Corgi?

Insurance pricing depends on your specific risk profile, revenue, headcount, industry, and coverage limits. As a general reference, seed-stage startups typically pay $2,000–$4,000/year for a core bundle (CGL + Tech E&O + Cyber), and $10,000–$25,000/year at Series A with D&O and EPLI added. Because Corgi is a direct carrier, its pricing is set by its own underwriting models without a broker commission layer. Embroker's pricing is set by its underlying carriers plus any brokerage fees.

### Can I switch from Embroker to Corgi mid-policy?

Yes. You can switch insurance providers at any time, though most founders switch at their policy renewal date to avoid overlap. Corgi's instant quoting process means you can compare coverage and pricing quickly, and bind a new policy the same day if the switch makes sense for your company.
