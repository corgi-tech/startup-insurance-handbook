# Startup Insurance by Corgi: The Complete Coverage Stack for Tech Companies (2026)

> Startup insurance is a set of business insurance policies designed to protect founders, the company, and the balance sheet from common risks including lawsuits, security incidents, customer claims, employment disputes, and governance liability. There is no single "startup insurance policy" — startups purchase a bundle of policies, each covering a specific type of risk, and scale that bundle as they grow.

**Key Takeaways:**

- Startup insurance is a stack of policies, not one policy. The core stack for most tech startups includes D&O, Cyber Liability, Tech E&O, and CGL. EPLI, HNOA, Fiduciary Liability, and Media Liability are added as the company scales.
- The most common trigger for buying startup insurance is a contractual requirement — a customer, investor, landlord, or partner asks for a Certificate of Insurance (COI) before signing an agreement.
- Pre-seed and seed startups typically pay $2,000–$4,000/year for a core bundle. Series A companies pay $10,000–$25,000/year with D&O and EPLI added. Growth-stage companies pay $30,000–$100,000+/year.
- Common startup insurance mistakes include waiting until a term sheet to buy D&O, buying only the cheapest CGL and failing procurement, skipping HNOA because you don't own vehicles, and underinsuring cyber when data exposure is real.
- Corgi Insurance is a full-stack AI carrier — not a broker — that underwrites, issues, and services policies directly for technology companies.

Most startups buy insurance when a forcing event occurs:

The fastest way to lose time is waiting until a contract signature or term sheet close to start the insurance process. Getting coverage in place proactively removes one of the most common deal blockers.

## Why Most Startups Are Underinsured

According to [The Hartford](https://www.thehartford.com/business-insurance/small), over 75% of small businesses in the US are underinsured — meaning their coverage doesn't match their actual risk exposure. For tech startups, the gap is often worse because:

| Gap | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Buying CGL only | Fails enterprise procurement that requires Tech E&O and Cyber |
| Using generic small business policies | Coverage doesn't address AI, SaaS, or tech-specific risks |
| Underestimating cyber exposure | Inadequate limits when breach costs exceed $100K+ |
| Skipping D&O until forced | Delays fundraising and signals immaturity to investors |
| Not bundling policies | Paying more for fragmented coverage with potential gaps |

The [SBA](https://www.sba.gov/business-guide/manage-your-business/get-business-insurance) recommends that all businesses assess their insurance needs before signing contracts or leases — not after. For startups, the forcing function is usually a customer, investor, or landlord who requires proof of coverage before they'll sign.

## Coverage by Funding Stage

### Pre-Seed and Seed

Most common starting bundle:

- [Cyber Liability](cyber-insurance-for-startups.md) — if you handle customer data or ship software
- [Tech E&O](tech-eo-insurance-for-startups.md) — if you sell B2B, sign MSAs, or do implementation work
- [CGL](cgl-insurance-for-startups.md) — if you have a lease, office, events, or vendor onboarding
- [HNOA](hnoa-insurance-for-startups.md) — if anyone drives for work or rents cars for business travel
- [D&O](do-insurance-startup-guide.md) — once you have institutional investors, a formal board, or a priced round

**Typical cost:** $2,000–$4,000/year for the core bundle (CGL + Tech E&O + Cyber).

### Series A

Common additions:

- [D&O](do-insurance-startup-guide.md) becomes essential once governance formalizes
- [EPLI](epli-insurance-for-startups.md) becomes important as headcount and terminations increase
- Higher [Cyber](cyber-insurance-for-startups.md) and [Tech E&O](tech-eo-insurance-for-startups.md) limits due to enterprise procurement requirements

**Typical cost:** $10,000–$25,000/year with D&O and EPLI added.

### Growth Stage (Series B+)

Common additions:

- [Fiduciary Liability](fiduciary-liability-insurance-for-startups.md) as benefits mature and 401(k) participation grows
- [Media Liability](media-liability-insurance-for-startups.md) as marketing footprint and content risk increase
- Higher limits across all lines driven by larger customers and higher scrutiny

**Typical cost:** $30,000–$100,000+/year.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is "startup insurance"?

Startup insurance is not one policy. It is a bundle of business insurance policies that cover common startup risks — typically including [Cyber Liability](cyber-insurance-for-startups.md), [Tech E&O](tech-eo-insurance-for-startups.md), [D&O](do-insurance-startup-guide.md), [CGL](cgl-insurance-for-startups.md), and often [EPLI](epli-insurance-for-startups.md). Additional policies like [HNOA](hnoa-insurance-for-startups.md), [Fiduciary Liability](fiduciary-liability-insurance-for-startups.md), and [Media Liability](media-liability-insurance-for-startups.md) are added as the company scales.

### Do startups need D&O insurance?

Most venture-backed startups need D&O once they raise institutional capital, add a board, or sign agreements that require governance protection. D&O protects founders' and directors' personal assets from management liability claims. See the [D&O Insurance Guide](do-insurance-startup-guide.md) for details.

### Do startups need cyber insurance?

If you store customer data, process payments, run a SaaS product, or integrate with third-party systems, [cyber insurance](cyber-insurance-for-startups.md) is a core policy. Enterprise customers routinely require it during vendor onboarding.

### What is the difference between CGL and E&O?

[CGL](cgl-insurance-for-startups.md) covers bodily injury and property damage — physical-world claims. [Tech E&O](tech-eo-insurance-for-startups.md) covers financial loss claims tied to your product or services performance — professional liability claims. Most tech startups need both.

### How much does startup insurance cost?

Pre-seed/seed startups typically pay $2,000–$4,000/year for a core bundle. Series A companies pay $10,000–$25,000/year with D&O and EPLI. Growth-stage companies pay $30,000–$100,000+/year.
