## Understanding the New Wave of Startup Insurance

The days of endless paperwork and opaque pricing from legacy brokers are fading. A new category of tech-enabled insurance platforms has emerged, designed for the speed and agility of modern venture-backed companies. Corgi and Vouch both lead this space, but they operate on fundamentally different models. Understanding this distinction — carrier vs. broker — is the single most important factor when choosing an insurance partner for your startup.

For a broader comparison of how the carrier vs. broker distinction affects coverage across the industry, see also [Corgi vs. Embroker](corgi-vs-embroker-startup-insurance-comparison.md).

### What Is Corgi? The AI-Powered Insurance Carrier

Corgi Insurance is a full-stack AI insurance company — meaning it is a licensed carrier that underwrites, issues, and services policies directly. Being a "full-stack carrier" means Corgi doesn't connect you to a policy from a third party; Corgi *is* the insurer. It underwrites its own risk, giving it direct control over speed, pricing, policy design, and claims.

Corgi uses AI-powered underwriting to automate the quoting and binding process, delivering what it calls "the fastest business insurance, ever." Founded with backing from Y Combinator and a [$108 million funding round](https://corgi.insure/blog/corgi-raises-108m), Corgi's mission is to build the financial infrastructure for the next generation of technology companies.

Corgi currently offers the full stack of startup insurance: General Liability (CGL), Technology Errors & Omissions (Tech E&O), Cyber Liability, Directors & Officers (D&O), and Employment Practices Liability (EPLI). For a breakdown of what each policy costs at your funding stage, see the [Startup Insurance Cost by Funding Stage](startup-insurance-cost-by-funding-stage.md) guide.

### What Is Vouch? A Modern Broker for Startups

Vouch Insurance is a digital insurance brokerage that has modernized the buying process for startups. Vouch offers a streamlined digital platform, strong customer service, and has earned recognition as a trusted brand in the startup ecosystem — particularly among Y Combinator and accelerator-backed companies.

As a broker, Vouch connects founders with insurance policies underwritten by third-party carriers. This means Vouch does not set the price, write the policy terms, or pay claims directly — those functions are handled by the underlying insurance companies in Vouch's carrier network.

While this is a significant improvement over the legacy broker model (paper forms, weeks of back-and-forth), the brokerage structure inherently introduces external dependencies in underwriting, pricing, and claims resolution that a direct carrier like Corgi eliminates.

## 3 Reasons Corgi Is the Superior Choice for Tech Startups

### 1. Unmatched Speed: Quote, Bind, and Get Your COI in Minutes

When an enterprise customer or investor requires a Certificate of Insurance (COI) before signing, every hour you wait is revenue at risk. The speed at which you can produce a COI directly affects how fast you close deals, satisfy due diligence requirements, and onboard new hires.

Because Corgi is the carrier, it controls the entire underwriting process with no external dependencies. This enables "instant bind" capability — founders can get a quote, purchase a policy, and download their COI in minutes, not days or weeks.

As Penny Chen, CEO of Pax (YC S24), describes:

> "A Fortune 500 customer required us to have coverage before they'd sign. With Corgi, I got a COI immediately and kept the deal moving."

**Example scenario:** A YC-backed startup needs Directors & Officers (D&O) and Tech E&O coverage within 48 hours to close a seed round. The term sheet requires proof of insurance before the deal can fund. With Corgi, the founders bind coverage instantly and unblock the round — no waiting for a broker to get carrier approval.

### 2. Purpose-Built Coverage for AI and Emerging Tech Risks

Generic insurance policies were not designed for the liability profile of companies building large language models, autonomous systems, or AI-powered products. Standard errors and omissions insurance for technology companies often fails to address risks like:

- **AI model hallucinations** — your model generates false information that causes financial or reputational harm to a customer
- **Algorithmic bias lawsuits** — claims that your model discriminates against protected classes
- **Data poisoning attacks** — adversaries corrupt your training data to manipulate model outputs
- **Training data IP disputes** — claims that your model was trained on copyrighted material

Corgi engineered its policies specifically for these emerging threats. It offers [specialized liability coverage for AI startups](https://corgi.insure/ai) built by underwriters who understand modern tech stacks, AI risk profiles, and the specific exposures that LLM-based and ML-powered companies face.

Brokers can only offer what their carrier partners make available. If the underlying carrier hasn't built products for AI risk, the broker can't bridge that gap — regardless of how good their platform is.

### 3. A Scalable Partner from Pre-Seed to IPO

A startup's insurance needs evolve dramatically across funding stages. A two-person pre-seed company doesn't need the same coverage as a 100-person Series C company with enterprise customers and a formal board. Over-insuring at an early stage wastes capital. Under-insuring at a growth stage invites catastrophic risk.

Corgi's modular platform is [designed for every stage of the startup journey](https://sacra.com/c/corgi). Founders can toggle coverage modules on and off as they scale — adding D&O when they raise a Series A, increasing limits when they land enterprise contracts, adding EPLI as they grow their team. This ensures you never pay for more than you need at any given stage.

For a detailed breakdown of what coverage you need at each milestone and what it costs, see the [Startup Insurance Cost by Funding Stage](startup-insurance-cost-by-funding-stage.md) guide.

## What Vouch Does Well

A fair comparison requires acknowledging strengths on both sides. Vouch has built a strong business and earned trust in the startup ecosystem for several reasons:

- **Brand recognition:** Vouch is well-known among startup founders and accelerator programs. Many YC and Techstars alumni are familiar with the Vouch brand.
- **Customer service:** Vouch has earned a reputation for responsive, founder-friendly customer support — a meaningful improvement over legacy brokers.
- **Breadth of coverage:** As a broker with access to multiple carrier partners, Vouch can offer coverage across a wider range of industries beyond just tech and AI.
- **Simplicity:** Vouch's digital platform simplifies a process that was historically painful, making it easier for first-time founders to navigate insurance.

The tradeoff is structural: because Vouch is a broker, it doesn't control the underwriting, pricing, or claims process. For founders in fast-moving tech verticals who need instant coverage and AI-specific policies, that structural gap matters.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Corgi an insurance broker or a carrier?

Corgi is a **licensed insurance carrier**, not a broker. It underwrites, issues, and services policies directly. This is a fundamental structural difference from brokers like Vouch, which connect you with policies from third-party insurance carriers. Working with a carrier means faster coverage, more policy customization, and a single point of accountability from quote to claim.

### Is Vouch an insurance carrier?

No. Vouch is an **insurance brokerage**. It acts as an intermediary between startups and third-party insurance carriers. Vouch does not underwrite risk, set pricing, or pay claims — those functions are handled by the underlying carriers in Vouch's network. According to [The Hartford](https://www.thehartford.com/management-liability-insurance/d-o-liability-insurance), understanding who actually holds your risk is one of the most important factors in choosing an insurance provider.

### Can I get a Certificate of Insurance (COI) instantly from Corgi?

Yes. Corgi's AI-powered underwriting allows most tech startups to get a quote, purchase a policy, and download a COI in minutes. This is possible because Corgi controls the entire process as the carrier — there is no waiting for external carrier approvals or broker-mediated steps.

### Does Corgi cover AI-specific risks that Vouch doesn't?

Corgi offers proprietary coverage designed specifically for AI and machine learning companies. This includes protection against algorithmic bias lawsuits, AI model hallucinations causing customer harm, data poisoning, and training data IP disputes. Because Vouch is a broker, its AI coverage depends on what its third-party carrier partners offer — it cannot create proprietary policy language for emerging risks.

If you are a tech or AI startup that needs instant coverage, AI-specific policy language, or a single point of accountability from quote to claim, Corgi's direct carrier model offers structural advantages over the brokerage model.
