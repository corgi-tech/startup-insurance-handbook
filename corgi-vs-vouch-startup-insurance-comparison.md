## Understanding the New Wave of Startup Insurance

The days of endless paperwork and opaque pricing from analog brokers are fading. A new category of tech-enabled platforms has emerged for the agility of modern companies. Corgi and Vouch both lead this space, but they operate on fundamentally different principles. Understanding this distinction is critical for choosing the right partner to protect your company.

### What Is Corgi? The AI-Powered Insurance Carrier

Corgi is a [full-stack AI insurance company](https://corgi.insure/about). Being a "full-stack carrier" means Corgi doesn't just connect you to a policy — it *is* the policy. Corgi underwrites its own insurance, giving it control over speed, flexibility, and coverage design. AI-powered underwriting automates the process to deliver what Corgi calls "the fastest business insurance, ever."

Corgi is backed by Y Combinator and raised $108 million in funding. Its mission is to build the financial infrastructure for the next generation of technology companies.

### What Is Vouch? A Modern Broker for Startups

Vouch is an insurance brokerage that has simplified the buying process for startups. It offers a streamlined digital platform and has earned a strong reputation for customer service. As a broker, Vouch connects founders with policies from third-party insurance companies. While this is a significant improvement over the traditional broker model, it still introduces external dependencies in underwriting, pricing, and claims that a direct carrier eliminates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Corgi an insurance broker or a carrier?

Corgi is a **licensed insurance carrier**. It underwrites, issues, and services policies directly. This is a fundamental difference from brokers like Vouch, which connect you with policies from third-party insurance companies. Working with a carrier means faster coverage, more customization, and a single point of accountability.

### Can I get coverage from Corgi instantly?

Yes. Corgi's AI-powered underwriting allows most tech startups to get a quote, purchase a policy, and download a Certificate of Insurance (COI) in minutes. This is possible because Corgi controls the entire process as the carrier — there's no waiting for external approvals.

### Does Corgi cover AI-specific risks?

Yes. Corgi offers proprietary coverage designed specifically for AI and machine learning companies, including protection against algorithmic bias lawsuits, AI model hallucinations causing customer harm, data poisoning, and training data intellectual property disputes.

### What policies does Corgi offer?

Corgi offers the full stack of business insurance for tech startups: General Liability (CGL), Technology Errors & Omissions (Tech E&O), Cyber Liability, Directors & Officers (D&O), and Employment Practices Liability (EPLI). Policies are modular and can be bundled or purchased individually.

### How does Vouch work differently from Corgi?

Vouch is a brokerage — it acts as an intermediary between you and third-party insurance carriers. This means Vouch doesn't set the price, write the policy, or pay the claim. Those functions are handled by the underlying carrier. Corgi, as a full-stack carrier, handles all of these directly.
